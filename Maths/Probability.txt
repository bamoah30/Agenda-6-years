Consider A and B are events
The probability of either event A or event B occurring is given by the formula:
P(A or B)= P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B) [Addition Rule]

Mutually Exclusive Events are events that do not occur at the same time
For Mutaully excluisve events, P(A and B) = 0

Independent events are events that the ouccurrnace of on event has any influence on the other event.
For independent events the probability of getting some events in a sample space is the multiplication of the their individual probabilities.

P(A and B) = P(A) \* P(B) [Multiplication Rule]

Expected Value / Mean

The expected value is the average outcome of an experiment when repeated many times.
It represents the long-run behavior of a random variable by weighting each possible
result with its probability.

Discrete Random Variable Formula:
E(X) = Î£ [ x_i * P(x_i) ]
where x_i are the possible outcomes and P(x_i) is the probability of each outcome.
