Heating Effect of Current:
-If the circuit is purely resistive, the electrical energy continuously gets dissipated entirely in the form of heat.

Heat = Power x time
H = P x t
H = VIt
H = (V^2/R)t

Joule's Law of Heating:
H = (I^2)Rt  

-Unit : joule, ev, kwh

-1J = 1watt-sec
1kwh= 1000*3600 watt-s
=> 1kwh = 3.6 x 10^6 J

Magnetic effect of electric Current:
A conducting material acts like a magent when current flows through it.

Notes:
Ferromagnetic materials: Strongly attracted to magent, usually solid and temperature increase causes decrease in magnetic property.

Pole Strength(m): Capacity of attraction and repulsion by poles.
-Equal for both poles, represented by "m" unit A-m

Magnetic dipole moment(M) = Poles Strength * distance between poles
unit A-m^2

Magnetic Force, F = (µ_o * m_1 *m_2)/(4pi*r^2)
where: µ_o=  permeability of vacuum
        µ_r = relative permeability
        m_1, m_2 =mass of body one and two, respectively
        µ = µ_oµ_r
        µ_o = 4pi x 10^7 N/A^2

Intensity of magnetic field:
-perpendicular force on pole strength of North pole
B = F/m => F= m*B

-vector quantity 
-unit N/(A-m) or Tesla
-Direction: North pole: outwards
            south pole: inwards


Magnetic Flux:
- Number of magnetic field lines passing throguh a unit area perpendicular to magnetic field.
phi = B dot A 
    = BA cos(theta)

-Scalar quantity
unit: T-m^2 or weber(wb)


Faraday's laws of EMI:
first law: Change in value of phi_B causes induced current and induced emf
second law: Induced emf is directly proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux.

Thus; e = Ndphi/dt 
where N = number of turns in winding 


Lenz law: Induced emf opposes the cause of generation
Thus: e = -Ndphi/dt
